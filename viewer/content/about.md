# About AdaptBase

AdaptBase is an open knowledge framework for urban climate adaptation — a structured ontology that maps the entities, relationships, and vocabularies needed to organize, compare, and analyze how cities respond to climate hazards.

## What this is

This viewer displays the AdaptBase ontology: {{TYPE_COUNT}} entity types (Solution, Hazard, Plan, Stakeholder, and more) connected by {{REL_COUNT}} typed relationships. The ontology provides a shared semantic layer for evidence on adaptation solutions drawn from published literature, city action plans, and project databases.

## How to use it

- **Explore the graph** — click any node to inspect its definition, properties, and relationships. Click an edge to read about that relationship type.
- **Search** — type in the search bar above the graph to jump directly to any entity or relationship.
- **Review & comment** — select a node or edge and open the Comments tab to read or leave structured feedback.

## What you can build with this

- **Knowledge graphs** — use the ontology as a schema to structure evidence from reports, plans, and datasets into queryable databases for agentic analysis.
- **Cross-city comparisons** — standardize how different municipalities describe solutions to identify patterns and transferable strategies.
- **Implementation trackers** — model the connections between solutions, stakeholders, funding, and outcomes to understand what works.
- **AI research tools** — provide semantic structure for LLMs analyzing adaptation literature or answering policy questions.
